# The 300-Year Window on Longevity, Lifespan, Healthspan & Aging

🏺 last300years v1.0.0 · window 1726–2026 · 8 survivors ranked

**Method note.** This is a *civilizational survival test*, not a highlight reel.
An item earns a place only with evidence that it stayed load-bearing across
**≥3 human generations** and survived at least one **paradigm/substrate shift**
that could have killed it. Ranking is by multi-generational endurance, never by
launch buzz. Guarded against Whig history: where an idea nearly died, the
near-death is reported as the finding; where authorship is contested, it is
marked contested rather than adjudicated. Four probes per item — P1 still
load-bearing today, P2 crossed ≥3 eras, P3 survived a named shift, P4
historian-attested (secondary source, not lore/listicle). Tiers: 🏛 canonical
(4/4) · 🌳 enduring (3/4) · ⚪ unproven.

**The central irony of this window.** Human life expectancy at birth roughly
*doubled* (from the high-30s to the high-70s/80s in rich countries) between 1726
and 2026 — one of the largest changes in the human condition ever recorded. Yet
almost none of that gain came from treating *aging*. It came from stopping people
dying *young* — of infection, in childbirth, in infancy. The survivors below are
overwhelmingly the machinery that defeated **premature death**. Aging itself
(cluster 7–8) remains, at the close of the window, essentially unsolved. Holding
those two facts together is the whole point of this document.

## Ranked Survival Clusters

### 1. Sanitation & the preventive / public-health idea — born 1842 (Chadwick), age 184 🏛
The single largest contributor to the doubling of lifespan was not a drug — it was
clean water, sewers, and the *idea that death is preventable by changing the
environment*. Edwin Chadwick's *Report on the Sanitary Condition of the Labouring
Population* (1842) proved life expectancy was far lower in towns than countryside
and — decisively — drew "a clear distinction between treatment and prevention,"
arguing disease "could be largely prevented" by public works, not medicine.
- **P1 load-bearing:** the environment-determines-health principle "continues to
  underpin public health practices today" ([PMC/NCBI on Chadwick](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11457289/)).
- **P2 generations:** Chadwick 1842 → Public Health Act 1848 → 20th-c. water/sewer
  systems → 21st-c. WASH programs and social-determinants epidemiology. ≥4 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** predates germ theory, yet *strengthened* after it — the
  "sanitary idea" was right for the wrong reasons (miasma) and the mechanism was
  swapped underneath it without discarding the practice.
- **P4 historian:** [BU School of Public Health, "The Sanitary Idea"](https://sphweb.bumc.bu.edu/otlt/mph-modules/ep/ep713_history/EP713_History7.html).
- **Voices:** "the various forms of epidemic, endemic, and other disease… are
  produced… by atmospheric impurities produced by decomposing… substances, by
  damp and filth, and close and overcrowded dwellings" (Chadwick, 1842) ↔ due to
  these reforms "average life expectancy in England increased by nearly 20 years
  within three generations" ([PMC 2024 review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11457289/)).
- **Lindy read:** 184 years old and the mechanism-swap-proof of its survival is
  exceptional. Expect it to outlive every pharmaceutical on this list.

### 2. Germ theory of disease — born ~1860s (Pasteur) / 1884 (Koch's postulates), age ~160 🏛
Louis Pasteur (1860s) and Robert Koch (1870s–80s) replaced "bad air" and
spontaneous generation with the claim that *specific microorganisms cause specific
diseases*. This is the substrate on which antibiotics, vaccination science,
sterile surgery, and clinical microbiology all stand.
- **P1 load-bearing:** "germ theory of disease is the foundation of modern medical
  practices and clinical microbiology" ([EBSCO Research Starters](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/biology/germ-theory-disease)).
- **P2 generations:** Pasteur/Koch → 20th-c. antibiotics & sterile technique →
  21st-c. genomic pathogen surveillance / PCR / COVID sequencing. ≥3 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** destroyed spontaneous generation and miasma theory
  outright — it *is* the paradigm shift, and it in turn survived the molecular/
  genomic revolution intact.
- **P4 historian:** [Britannica, "Germ theory"](https://www.britannica.com/science/germ-theory); [NCBI Bookshelf, "A Theory of Germs"](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK24649/).
- **Voices:** Koch's demonstration that "a specific germ caused a specific disease"
  (anthrax, 1876) ↔ "Regarded as the most important discovery in the history of
  medicine" and still "the foundation of modern medical practices" (21st-c.
  encyclopedic consensus, [Encyclopedia.com](https://www.encyclopedia.com/science/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/germ-theory)).
- **Lindy read:** canonical. The enduring *principle* — one specific cause → one
  specific intervention (Koch's postulates) — is also, tellingly, the principle
  that has *failed* for aging (see Gaps).

### 3. Vaccination / immunization — born 1796 (Jenner), age 230 🏛
Edward Jenner's 1796 cowpox inoculation of James Phipps was "the world's first
successful vaccine" and founded immunology. It is the only human discipline that
has ever *eradicated* a disease (smallpox, declared 1980).
- **P1 load-bearing:** vaccines remain a front-line healthspan technology; the
  discipline Jenner founded produced the mRNA platforms of the 2020s.
- **P2 generations:** Jenner 1796 → Pasteur's attenuated vaccines 1880s → 20th-c.
  polio/measles → 21st-c. mRNA. ≥4 eras, each re-adopting the core principle.
- **P3 shift survived:** invented ~65 years *before* germ theory explained why it
  worked; the mechanism (adaptive immunity) was discovered underneath it without
  discarding the practice — a textbook substrate change survived.
- **P4 historian:** [WHO, "A Brief History of Vaccination"](https://www.who.int/news-room/spotlight/history-of-vaccination/a-brief-history-of-vaccination); [PMC, "Jenner's Legacy"](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11450354/).
- **Voices:** Jenner's prediction that "the annihilation of the smallpox… must be
  the final result of this practice" (1801) ↔ WHO's formal declaration that
  smallpox eradication was achieved in 1980 — a 179-year-old prediction confirmed.
- **Lindy read:** 230 years and the *only* eradication in history. Canonical.

### 4. Antibiotics — born 1928 (Fleming) / 1941–43 (Florey, Chain, Heatley, clinical), age ~98 / ~84 🌳
Fleming's 1928 observation of *Penicillium* → the Oxford team's wartime
mass-production turned bacterial infection from a common cause of death into a
usually-curable event. Downgraded from 🏛 to 🌳 for one reason: **it is actively
eroding.**
- **P1 load-bearing:** penicillin G/V "are still used clinically" (syphilis, strep,
  rheumatic-fever prophylaxis) ([Britannica, penicillin](https://www.britannica.com/science/penicillin)).
- **P2 generations:** 1940s wonder-drug era → 1960s–80s broad-spectrum expansion →
  21st-c. resistance-constrained stewardship. ≥3 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** survived the transition from natural to semi-synthetic to
  synthetic chemistry — but is now being *un-made* by antimicrobial resistance
  (AMR), which appeared within four years of mass production.
- **P4 historian:** [CDC/EID, "The Discovery of Penicillin—New Insights After More Than 75 Years"](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/eid/article/23/5/16-1556_article); [Johns Hopkins, "A Brief History of Antibiotic Resistance"](https://magazine.publichealth.jhu.edu/2025/brief-history-antibiotic-resistance).
- **Voices:** Fleming's 1945 Nobel warning that "the time may come when penicillin
  can be bought by anyone in the shops… [and] the ignorant man may easily
  underdose himself and… make [microbes] resistant" ↔ "Just four years after drug
  companies began mass-producing penicillin in 1943, microbes began appearing that
  could resist it" (JHU, 2025). The inventor forecast the erosion at the moment of
  triumph.
- **Lindy read:** the load-bearing *principle* (small molecules can selectively
  kill pathogens) endures; specific drugs are mortal. The AMR story is the single
  clearest proof in this document that **a civilizational win requires stewardship,
  not just a one-time discovery** — a lesson longevity science has not yet had to
  learn, but will.

### 5. Nutrition, deficiency diseases & the controlled trial — born 1747 (Lind) / 1912 (Funk), age 279 / 114 🏛
Two survivors braided together. First, James Lind's 1747 scurvy experiment on HMS
*Salisbury* — twelve sailors "as similar as I could have them," six paired
treatments, only citrus worked — is repeatedly attested as *the first controlled
clinical trial*, encoding the enduring principle of the **fair test / controlled
comparison**. Second, the *deficiency-disease* paradigm: Casimir Funk's 1912
"vitamine" concept explained *why* Lind's citrus worked and founded nutritional
science.
- **P1 load-bearing:** the controlled comparison is the spine of every modern RCT
  and evidence-based medicine; vitamins/micronutrient science underpins global
  food fortification today.
- **P2 generations:** Lind 1747 → 19th-c. naval/army rations → Funk 1912 vitamins →
  Bradford Hill's 1948 streptomycin RCT → 21st-c. evidence-based medicine. ≥4 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** the *method* survived the pre→post-statistics substrate
  change (Lind used no statistics; the identical logic was formalized by Fisher and
  Bradford Hill two centuries later without discarding Lind's design).
- **P4 historian:** [The James Lind Library](https://www.jameslindlibrary.org/articles/james-lind-and-scurvy-1747-to-1795/); [PMC, "A brief history of vitamin C"](https://www.mv.helsinki.fi/home/hemila/history/).
- **Voices:** Lind's report shows "his awareness of the need to guard against
  selection bias… holding potential confounding factors constant" (1747, per James
  Lind Library) ↔ "Subsequent research confirmed most of Funk's opinions…
  establishing the theoretical framework that explained why Lind's citrus cure had
  worked centuries earlier" (modern review). The 165-year gap between demonstration
  and explanation *is* the survival evidence.
- **Lindy read:** the controlled trial at 279 is arguably the most durable single
  idea here — and the one modern n=1 "biohacking" longevity culture most often
  ignores.

### 6. Epidemiology & the actuarial / demographic law of mortality — born 1825 (Gompertz) / 1854 (Snow), age ~201 / ~172 🏛
The quantitative understanding of *how populations die*. John Snow's 1854 Broad
Street investigation (mapping cases, removing the pump handle) founded modern
**epidemiology**; Benjamin Gompertz's 1825 discovery that adult mortality rises
*exponentially* with age gave aging its first mathematical law. Together with
William Farr's vital-statistics apparatus, this is *denominator thinking* — you
cannot fight mortality you cannot count. (Note: the deeper root, John Graunt's
1662 *Bills of Mortality* and Halley's 1693 life table, predates the 1726 window —
window overflow, flagged, not scored.)
- **P1 load-bearing:** the Gompertz–Makeham law "is widely used in actuarial
  science… demography and gerontology… and reliability theory of ageing," and
  epidemiology is the operating system of public health ([Gompertz–Makeham, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gompertz%E2%80%93Makeham_law_of_mortality)).
- **P2 generations:** Gompertz 1825 → Makeham 1860 extension → 20th-c. life
  insurance & demography → 21st-c. biogerontology mortality modeling. ≥4 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** survived the entire transition from hand-computed life
  tables to computational demography; the 200-year-old functional form is still
  fitted to modern data.
- **P4 historian:** [Royal Society, "Deciphering death: a commentary on Gompertz (1825)"](https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rstb/article/370/1666/20140379/22564/Deciphering-death-a-commentary-on-Gompertz-1825-On); Snow attested "founding father of Epidemiology" ([Gavi](https://www.gavi.org/vaccineswork/john-snow-and-pump-handle-public-health)).
- **Voices:** Gompertz on "the nature of the function expressive of the law of
  human mortality" (1825 title) ↔ "Approaching its bicentennial, the law remains
  highly influential across… demography, biology, gerontology, economics, and
  actuarial science" ([The Actuary, 2025](https://www.theactuary.com/2025/03/24/mortality-man-influence-benjamin-gompertz)).
- **Lindy read:** canonical. The Gompertz curve is the closest thing aging has to a
  law of physics — and, crucially, it is what any aging *biomarker* must ultimately
  predict (see Gaps).

### 7. The evolutionary theory of aging — roots ~1880s–1940s, crystallized 1952 (Medawar) / 1957 (Williams), age ~85–140 (crystallization ~70) 🌳
*Why* we age, in the only framework that has survived scrutiny: aging results from
the **declining force of natural selection with age**. August Weismann's 1880s
"programmed death for the good of the species" was the loud early theory — and it
was *wrong* and abandoned. The durable version came from Fisher (1930) and Haldane
(1941) on declining reproductive value, formalized by Medawar (mutation
accumulation, 1952) and Williams (antagonistic pleiotropy, 1957), later Hamilton
(1966) and Kirkwood's disposable-soma (1977).
- **P1 load-bearing:** "modern versions of Medawar's and William's evolutionary
  theories of aging are still widely believed today by most experts in aging
  science"; antagonistic-pleiotropy trade-offs are "common, perhaps ubiquitous"
  ([Max Planck Inst. for Biology of Ageing](https://www.age.mpg.de/why-do-we-age); [Oxford EMPH 2018](https://academic.oup.com/emph/article/2018/1/287/5126836)).
- **P2 generations:** Weismann 1880s (rejected) → Fisher/Haldane 1930s–40s →
  Medawar/Williams 1950s → 21st-c. genomic tests of pleiotropy. ≥3 eras of the
  *durable* lineage.
- **P3 shift survived:** survived the discrediting of Weismann's programmed-aging
  paradigm — the field *replaced* its own founding theory, which is exactly the
  kind of near-death this method flags rather than hides.
- **P4 historian:** [Nature Scitable, "The Evolution of Aging"](https://www.nature.com/scitable/knowledge/library/the-evolution-of-aging-23651151/).
- **Voices:** Medawar's framing that "the force of natural selection weakens with
  increasing age" (1952) ↔ "These tradeoffs, known as antagonistic pleiotropy, are
  common, perhaps ubiquitous" (2018 genetic review). Two generations of voices, one
  surviving idea.
- **Lindy read (with window-edge caveat):** the *papers* Medawar 1952 / Williams
  1957 are ~70 yr — just under the 75-yr bar, which by strict rule redirects to
  /last30years. But the *principle* (senescence = declining selection) is
  ≥85–140 yr through its Weismann/Fisher/Haldane roots and is load-bearing today,
  so it earns 🌳 in-window. Its practical warning — **there is no single "aging
  gene" to switch off; expect trade-offs** — is the principle current
  single-target longevity biotech most often ignores.

### 8. Caloric restriction — the one robust experimental lever on aging — born 1935 (McCay), age 91 🌳
Clive McCay's 1935 rats showed that reducing calorie intake (while maintaining
nutrition) extended lifespan ~30–40%. Nearly a century later it remains "the only
intervention shown convincingly to slow aging" across taxa from yeast to primates
in the lab — the empirical bedrock of biogerontology.
- **P1 load-bearing:** CR "has emerged… to become one of the most widely used tools
  of biogerontologists for dissecting biological mechanisms of aging" ([ScienceDirect review](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304416509000385)).
- **P2 generations:** McCay 1935 → Walford/Weindruch 1980s mechanistic era →
  21st-c. mTOR/sirtuin/rapamycin mimetic programs. ≥3 eras.
- **P3 shift survived:** survived the molecular-biology revolution — instead of
  being discarded it became the *assay* against which molecular theories (mTOR,
  IGF-1, sirtuins) are tested.
- **P4 historian:** [Scientific American, "Calorie Restriction and Aging"](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/calorie-restriction-and-aging/); [PMC, McCay caveats review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5565679/).
- **Voices:** McCay et al.'s finding that "retardation of growth" by restricted
  feeding markedly extends life (1935) ↔ "Since the 1930s, calorie restriction has
  been the only intervention shown convincingly to slow aging in rodents… and in
  creatures ranging from single-celled protozoans to roundworms, fruit flies and
  fish" (Scientific American).
- **Lindy read:** 🌳 enduring in the lab — but its **failure to translate cleanly to
  human maximum lifespan** is itself one of the defining Gaps. It endures as a
  *scientific paradigm*, not yet as a *human intervention*.

## Enduring Pillars (the survivor list)

1. **Sanitation & the preventive/public-health idea** (Chadwick 1842) — the biggest single driver of the lifespan doubling.
2. **Germ theory of disease** (Pasteur/Koch, 1860s–80s) — the substrate of modern medicine.
3. **Vaccination / immunology** (Jenner 1796) — the only discipline to eradicate a disease.
4. **Antibiotics** (Fleming 1928 / Florey–Chain–Heatley 1940s) — eroding under AMR.
5. **Nutrition / deficiency-disease science + the controlled trial** (Lind 1747, Funk 1912).
6. **Epidemiology & the actuarial/demographic law of mortality** (Snow 1854, Gompertz 1825, Farr).
7. **The evolutionary theory of aging** (Weismann→Fisher/Haldane→Medawar 1952/Williams 1957).
8. **Caloric restriction** (McCay 1935) — the one reproducible lab lever on aging rate.

## Enduring Principles (what the pillars encode)

- **Prevention beats cure.** The largest lifespan gains came from stopping disease, not treating it (Chadwick).
- **Denominator / population thinking.** You cannot fight mortality you cannot count; measure the base rate first (Graunt→Farr→Gompertz→Snow).
- **Mortality is lawful and quantifiable.** Aging has a mathematical signature (Gompertz) — any real intervention must bend that curve.
- **One specific cause → one specific intervention** (Koch's postulates) — spectacularly successful for infection, and spectacularly *insufficient* for aging.
- **The fair test.** Controlled comparison, guarding against confounding and bias, is the arbiter of truth (Lind→Fisher→Bradford Hill).
- **Cheap, scalable, equitably-distributed public goods beat heroic private interventions.** Clean water and vaccines, not elite cures, moved the population needle.
- **Mechanism can be swapped under a working practice.** Vaccination and sanitation both worked before their mechanism was known — durable *practices* outlive the *theories* that justify them.
- **Aging is shaped by the declining force of selection, not by purpose.** No single "aging program" or master gene; expect antagonistic trade-offs (Medawar/Williams).
- **A single environmental lever can reproducibly change the rate of aging** (McCay/CR) — biology of aging is manipulable in principle.
- **A civilizational win requires stewardship, not just discovery.** AMR shows even the greatest wins erode without maintenance.

## Gaps (civilizational window)

Century-scale problems that persisted despite everything — and the enduring
principles a modern **AI-native** longevity effort should honor but current work
routinely ignores.

- **Aging itself is unsolved.** After 300 years of doubling *life expectancy at
  birth*, there is still **no intervention documented to extend maximum human
  lifespan**; the ~122-year ceiling stands ([PMC, "No limit to maximal lifespan… 122-year record"](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8636159/)). Every survivor above fought *premature death*; none conquered senescence.

- **The healthspan–lifespan gap is widening.** We added years of life but not years
  of health: the global gap grew from ~8.5 yr (2000) to ~9.6 yr (2020) and is
  still expanding ([JAMA/PMC, global healthspan-lifespan gap](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC11635540/); [npj Regen Med, "mind the healthspan gap"](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41536-021-00169-5)). Medicine learned to extend *dying* better than it extended *living* — the exact opposite of Fries' 1980 "compression of morbidity" ideal, which remains contested and unrealized.

- **We still cannot measure aging.** Gompertz gave mortality a law in 1825, yet in
  2026 there is **no validated biomarker or "aging clock" accepted as a regulatory
  surrogate endpoint** for aging. Trials must still wait for death or disease as
  the readout — a measurement gap that blocks fast, cheap iteration. *Principle
  ignored: build the clock before the cure (denominator/measurement thinking).*

- **The translation gap.** The interventions that robustly slow aging in the lab
  (caloric restriction since 1935; rapamycin, IGF/mTOR manipulation in worms, flies,
  mice) **fail, dilute, or remain unproven in humans**. The one lever we have known
  longest — CR — does not translate cleanly to human maximum lifespan. *Principle
  ignored: model-organism success is a hypothesis, not a result — honor the fair
  test at human scale.*

- **Aging is not classified as a disease.** There is no approved indication for
  "aging," so capital, regulation, and trials route *around* the root cause toward
  single end-organ diseases — re-running Koch's "one cause → one drug" logic on a
  problem that is not monocausal. We keep treating the manifestations (heart, brain,
  joints) rather than the shared driver. *Principle ignored: match the paradigm to
  the problem — the reductionist single-target model that beat infection is the
  wrong tool for a networked, multi-causal process.*

- **The inequality gap — Chadwick's unfinished business.** Longevity gains are
  distributed along a steep social gradient; "every material social condition… is
  patterned by socioeconomic position" ([Milbank Quarterly, revisiting compression of morbidity](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7482385/)). Yet the biggest *historical* wins were cheap public goods (water, vaccines), while modern longevity effort concentrates on **expensive, private, individual** interventions. *Principle ignored: cheap-and-scalable-and-equitable beats heroic-and-private.*

- **The complexity / no-master-switch gap.** Evolutionary theory has warned since
  Medawar/Williams that aging has **no single program to switch off** and that
  life-extending changes carry antagonistic-pleiotropy trade-offs. Single-target
  "flip one gene / take one molecule" longevity biotech repeatedly ignores this.
  *Principle ignored: expect trade-offs and systems behavior, not silver bullets —
  precisely the regime where AI-native, multi-target, whole-system modeling could
  add value the 20th-century toolkit cannot.*

- **Erosion of past wins (the stewardship gap).** Even canonical pillars decay:
  antimicrobial resistance is un-making antibiotics, and vaccine hesitancy threatens
  immunization. A field obsessed with *new* discovery under-invests in **maintaining**
  the wins already banked. *Principle ignored: durability requires ongoing
  stewardship — a civilizational win is a maintained system, not a trophy.*

## The Anti-Portfolio (era-matched corpses)

Ideas that were *louder in their era* than several survivors above — and died.
Their graves are what make the ranking mean something.

- **Miasma theory ("bad air" causes disease)** — dominant through the mid-19th c.,
  more mainstream than Snow's waterborne hypothesis in 1854. Refuted by Snow +
  germ theory. Died — yet, tellingly, the *sanitation practices* it motivated
  survived (mechanism swapped, practice kept).
- **Spontaneous generation** — orthodox for millennia; killed decisively by
  Pasteur's experiments in the 1860s ([NCBI Bookshelf](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK24649/)).
- **Brown-Séquard organotherapy & Voronoff's "monkey gland" grafts (1889–1930s)** —
  the *loudest* longevity science of its era: Voronoff was "applauded by over 700
  scientists at the International Congress of Surgeons in London" (1923) and
  operated on thousands. By the 1940s "branded as bogus"; in 1952 dismissed as "no
  better than the methods of witches and magicians" ([Atlas Obscura](https://www.atlasobscura.com/articles/the-true-story-of-dr-voronoffs-plan-to-use-monkey-testicles-to-make-us-immortal); [History of Nephrology](https://historyofnephrology.org/general/monkey-glands-and-the-science-of-renal-hypertension/)). **The cautionary tale for every generation of rejuvenation hype, including our own.**
- **Metchnikoff's "intestinal autointoxication" / longevity-through-yogurt (~1900s)** —
  a Nobel laureate's theory that gut putrefaction caused aging, hugely popular; the
  specific mechanism was abandoned (though it dimly prefigured microbiome science).
- **Weismann's "programmed death for the good of the species" (1880s)** — the
  founding evolutionary theory of aging, later rejected in favor of the
  declining-force-of-selection account (see cluster 7). The field killed its own
  founder-theory.
- **Humoral medicine & routine bloodletting** — persisted into the 19th century,
  then abandoned as germ theory and the controlled trial exposed its harm.

## ⚪ Unproven this run (leads only — promising, not yet century-proven)

- **Rapamycin / mTOR inhibitors** for human longevity — strong in mice; human
  lifespan/healthspan effect unconfirmed.
- **Metformin (TAME trial lineage)** as a geroprotector — hypothesis-stage for
  healthy humans.
- **Senolytics** (clearing senescent cells) — compelling preclinical data; durable
  human benefit unproven.
- **NAD⁺ boosters (NMN/NR)** — popular, mechanistically motivated, human outcomes
  weak/mixed.
- **Partial epigenetic reprogramming (Yamanaka-factor age reversal)** — the highest-
  ceiling frontier idea; entirely preclinical, safety unknown.
- **The "Hallmarks of Aging" framework (2013)** — organizing and influential, but
  too young for a survival verdict; watch it.
- **Epigenetic/proteomic "aging clocks"** — the most important lead to watch,
  because a *validated* clock would resolve the measurement gap above — but none is
  yet a regulatory surrogate endpoint.
